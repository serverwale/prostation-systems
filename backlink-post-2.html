What to look for in an AI workstation

GPU and VRAM

VRAM is the first constraint in deep learning — your model and batch must fit in GPU memory. Size VRAM to the largest model you plan to train, not to the cheapest card. For multi-GPU setups, ensure the chassis has the PCIe lanes, power and cooling to run them at full load.

CPU, RAM and storage

The CPU feeds the GPUs and handles data preprocessing, so don't starve it. Pair it with plenty of ECC RAM (256 GB+ for large datasets) and fast NVMe storage so your data pipeline never becomes the bottleneck. A balanced custom-built workstation keeps the GPUs busy instead of waiting on disk or RAM.

Thermals and reliability

AI workloads pin hardware at 100% for hours. Proper airflow, quality power delivery and a warranty you can actually claim in India separate a workstation that lasts years from one that throttles or fails. Always confirm on-site or return-to-base support before buying.
